Australia Labour Force Visualization

This interactive web page presents visualizations of trends in the Australian labour force. Explore employment, unemployment, and workforce data through interactive maps and charts below.

Employment Trends by State

This map provides a snapshot of the employment levels across various states and territories in Australia as of January 2024. You can hover over any state to view specific employment numbers and unemployment rates. This visualization highlights the distribution of employment across the country, helping policymakers and analysts identify regions with stronger or weaker labour markets.

Monthly Hours Worked and Yearly Change

This line chart tracks the total monthly hours worked in Australia from 2014 to 2024. The chart also includes a bar chart showing the yearly percentage change, which allows for a clearer understanding of the fluctuations in the labour market. This visualization is useful for understanding long-term trends in workforce productivity and how external factors, such as economic events, affect the total hours worked.

Employment, Earnings, and Workforce Share by Industry

This dual-axis chart presents a breakdown of employment, earnings, and workforce share by industry. The bar chart represents the workforce share of different industries, while the line chart represents median weekly earnings. This visualization allows users to explore how different industries compare in terms of the number of employees and average earnings, providing valuable insights into the structure of the Australian labour market.

Industry Employment Breakdown

This bubble chart displays the relationship between the number of employees in various industries, median weekly earnings, and workforce share. Each bubble represents a different industry, with the size of the bubble corresponding to the number of employees, the x-axis representing earnings, and the y-axis representing workforce share. This chart helps in understanding which industries have the most significant impact on the labour market.

Part-Time and Full-Time Employment by Industry

This stacked bar chart compares part-time and full-time employment across different industries in Australia. It helps users understand which industries are more reliant on part-time workers and which are dominated by full-time roles. By analyzing this data, users can gain insights into the nature of employment across sectors and how it varies between industries.
